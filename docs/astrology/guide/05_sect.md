## The one-line version

**Sect** is whether you were born during the day or the night, and it quietly
re-sorts the entire chart into who's helping you and who's giving you trouble.
It's the most powerful idea in astrology that almost nobody teaches.

## Why this chapter matters more than it looks like it should

Modern pop astrology treats Mars and Saturn as generic bad guys: everyone's
Saturn is hard, everyone's Mars is trouble. That's wrong, and sect is why. Sect
tells you *which* of the two malefics is the genuinely difficult one for you
specifically, and which one is actually working on your behalf. Two people with
the identical Saturn placement can experience it as night and day, literally,
because one was born by day and the other by night. Once you see it, you can't
unsee it, and your readings get an entire extra dimension.

---

## A brief history

Sect was one of the *first* things a Hellenistic astrologer checked, right after
the rising sign. Before analyzing anything else, you established whether the chart
was diurnal (day) or nocturnal (night), because that single fact reorganized how
you read every planet. It was foundational, not optional.

Then it faded. Medieval astrology kept it but leaned harder on other techniques,
and by the time modern psychological astrology took over in the 20th century,
sect had been almost entirely dropped. A whole generation of astrologers learned
to read charts without ever hearing the word. This is why it feels like a secret:
it isn't esoteric, it was just *lost* from the mainstream for a few centuries.

It came back with the traditional revival. Robert Hand's small 1995 book *Night
and Day: Planetary Sect in Astrology* put it back on the map, and the Hellenistic
translation projects of the 1990s and 2000s restored it to the center of
traditional practice. If a technique in this guide feels like it's giving you
X-ray vision that horoscope-column astrology never mentioned, sect is usually the
reason.

## The theory

### Day or night: is the Sun up?

The rule is exactly as simple as it sounds. Look at where the Sun is relative to
the horizon at the moment of birth.

- Sun **above** the horizon (in the visible sky, houses 7 through 12): **day
  chart**, diurnal.
- Sun **below** the horizon (underfoot, houses 1 through 6): **night chart**,
  nocturnal.

That's it. Born at 2pm, day chart. Born at 2am, night chart. The Sun's position
above or below the horizon line (the Ascendant-Descendant axis from
[chapter 03](./03_houses.md)) is the whole determination.

### The two teams

Every planet belongs to one of two sects, day or night, like two teams that each
play better under their own lighting.

- **The day team (diurnal):** the **Sun**, **Jupiter**, and **Saturn**.
- **The night team (nocturnal):** the **Moon**, **Venus**, and **Mars**.
- **Mercury** switches sides depending on whether it rises before the Sun
  (diurnal) or after (nocturnal). It's the free agent.

Notice the logic. Each team has a light (Sun/Moon), a benefic (Jupiter/Venus),
and a malefic (Saturn/Mars). The hot, dry, harsh planets (Sun, Jupiter is warm,
and especially Saturn) are diurnal; the cool, moist ones (Moon, Venus, Mars) are
nocturnal. Saturn, the cold planet, is actually calmed by the *day*; Mars, the
hot planet, is cooled and steadied by the *night*. Hold that, because it drives
everything below.

### The sect light: your most important planet

In a **day** chart, the **Sun** is the *sect light*. In a **night** chart, the
**Moon** is. The sect light is the single most important point in the chart under
this doctrine, the luminary that's "in charge." Read it first, before anything
else.

> [!IMPORTANT]
> In a day chart, read the **Sun** first. In a night chart, read the **Moon**
> first. The sect light is the luminary running the show, and beginners
> consistently over-weight the Sun for everyone when half of all people are
> night-born and led by the Moon.

### In-sect and out-of-sect, and the malefic twist

A planet is **in sect** when its team matches the chart (day-team planets in a day
chart, night-team planets in a night chart) and **out of sect** otherwise. In
sect, a planet is in its comfortable lighting and behaves better. Out of sect, it
struggles against the conditions.

For the benefics this is intuitive: the **benefic of the sect** (Jupiter by day,
Venus by night) is the more helpful one, at full strength; the out-of-sect benefic
is still good, just dialed down.

For the malefics it's beautifully counterintuitive, and this is the payoff of the
whole chapter:

> [!NOTE]
> The **malefic of the sect** is the *better-behaved* one. A malefic in its
> preferred lighting does its hard job (Saturn's discipline, Mars's cut)
> constructively and on purpose. The **out-of-sect malefic** is the genuinely
> difficult one, doing damage against its own grain. So: **in-sect malefic =
> the tough-but-fair one; out-of-sect malefic = the one that actually hurts.**

Putting it together, the roles by sect:

| Role | Day chart | Night chart |
|---|---|---|
| Sect light (read first) | Sun | Moon |
| Benefic of the sect (best) | Jupiter | Venus |
| Out-of-sect benefic | Venus | Jupiter |
| Malefic of the sect (constructive) | Saturn | Mars |
| Out-of-sect malefic (the hard one) | Mars | Saturn |

The single most useful takeaway: **in a day chart, Mars is your harder malefic;
in a night chart, Saturn is.** Same two planets, opposite verdicts, decided by
one fact about the horizon.

## In practice

### The worked contrast: Marilyn Monroe (day) vs Muhammad Ali (night)

These two charts are a gift for learning sect, because they happen to share a
rising sign (both Leo rising), so the *only* big structural difference is that one
was born by day and one by night. Watch the malefics trade places.

**Marilyn Monroe, day chart, Leo rising.** Her sect light is the **Sun in Gemini
in the 10th**, angular and public, exactly right for the most photographed
identity of her century. Her benefic of the sect, **Jupiter, sits in the 7th** of
partnership. And her harder, **out-of-sect malefic is Mars, in the 8th house** of
sex, death, and other people's power and money. Sect flags her Mars as the
genuinely difficult planet, and it lands in the house of precisely the forces that
exploited and finally ended her. Her in-sect Saturn (in the 4th) is the
better-behaved malefic by comparison.

**Muhammad Ali, night chart, Leo rising.** His sect light is the **Moon in
Aquarius in the 6th** of work, the body, and service, fitting for a man whose life
was his physical training and his causes. His benefic of the sect is **Venus in
the 7th**. And here's the flip: his **Mars is in sect**, the *constructive*
malefic, sitting in the **9th house** of belief, principle, and the wider world. A
disciplined, purposeful warrior's Mars, aimed at faith and justice: the refusal to
be drafted, the conversion, the global fight for principle. His harder,
out-of-sect malefic is **Saturn, in the 10th**, the establishment and authority
that stripped his title and prosecuted him, the hard blocking force in his public
life.

Same rising sign. In one chart Mars is the wound; in the other Mars is the weapon.
That difference is sect, and nothing else.

### Read the sect light, then find the hard malefic

A fast, high-value first pass on any chart:

1. Day or night? (Is the Sun above or below the horizon?)
2. Read the **sect light** (Sun by day, Moon by night) as the chart's lead.
3. Identify the **out-of-sect malefic** (Mars by day, Saturn by night) and see
   what house and sign it's in. That's often where the person works hardest for
   the least thanks.
4. Lean on the **benefic of the sect** (Jupiter by day, Venus by night) as the
   most reliable help on offer.

### The boundary problem (and why Jung can't headline this chapter)

Sect depends entirely on the birth time, and near sunrise or sunset the Sun sits
right on the horizon, where "above or below" becomes a genuine coin-toss.

> [!WARNING]
> For births near sunrise or sunset, sect can flip with a few minutes of birth
> time, and flipping sect **reverses your entire malefic analysis** (the hard
> planet and the constructive one swap). This is the highest-stakes reason to nail
> down an accurate birth time. Our lead example, Jung, was born at sunset with
> C-rated data, so his sect is genuinely uncertain, which is exactly why this
> chapter is led by Monroe and Ali instead.

### Sect runs deeper than the malefics

The day/night split doesn't stop at benefics and malefics. It also drives:

- **Triplicity rulers:** each element has a *day* ruler and a *night* ruler, so
  sect decides which planet governs a sign's element for you
  ([chapter 06](./06_dignity_and_rulership.md)).
- **The Lots:** the Part of Fortune and Part of Spirit *swap their formulas*
  between day and night charts ([chapter 07](./07_lots_arabic_parts.md)).
- **Dignity scoring:** a planet gets credit for being in sect as part of its
  overall condition.

Stellium handles all of these sect-aware calculations for you automatically, which
is the next section.

## In Stellium

### Determining sect

```python
from stellium import ChartBuilder

chart = ChartBuilder.from_notable("Marilyn Monroe").calculate()
print(chart.sect())    # "day"

chart = ChartBuilder.from_notable("Muhammad Ali").calculate()
print(chart.sect())    # "night"
```

> [!TIP]
> `chart.sect()` is a **method**, so call it with parentheses. `chart.sect`
> without them returns the bound method, not the answer.

### Reading the sect roles off any chart

Sect roles are pure logic once you know day or night, so a tiny helper turns any
chart into the table from above:

```python
from stellium import ChartBuilder

DAY   = [("Sun","sect light"), ("Jupiter","benefic of sect"),
         ("Venus","out-of-sect benefic"), ("Saturn","malefic of sect"),
         ("Mars","out-of-sect malefic (the hard one)")]
NIGHT = [("Moon","sect light"), ("Venus","benefic of sect"),
         ("Jupiter","out-of-sect benefic"), ("Mars","malefic of sect"),
         ("Saturn","out-of-sect malefic (the hard one)")]

def sect_roles(name):
    c = ChartBuilder.from_notable(name).calculate()
    roles = DAY if c.sect() == "day" else NIGHT
    print(f"{name} ({c.sect()} chart):")
    for planet, role in roles:
        o = c.get_object(planet)
        print(f"  {planet:8} {o.sign_position:20} house {c.get_house(planet):<2}  {role}")

sect_roles("Muhammad Ali")
# Muhammad Ali (night chart):
#   Moon     12°25' Aquarius      house 6   sect light
#   Venus    20°40' Aquarius      house 7   benefic of sect
#   Jupiter  11°57' Gemini        house 10  out-of-sect benefic
#   Mars      3°03' Taurus        house 9   malefic of sect
#   Saturn   21°39' Taurus        house 10  out-of-sect malefic (the hard one)
```

### Sect-aware calculations happen automatically

You don't have to tell Stellium the sect for dignities or lots; it reads it from
the chart. The triplicity rulers in the dignity system
([chapter 06](./06_dignity_and_rulership.md)) and the Fortune/Spirit formulas in
the lots ([chapter 07](./07_lots_arabic_parts.md)) both flip correctly on their
own based on `chart.sect()`. The [dignities
cookbook](../../../examples/dignities_cookbook.py) shows the sect-aware dignity
scoring end to end.
